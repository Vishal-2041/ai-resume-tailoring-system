🤖 What is an ATS?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software companies use to collect, filter, and manage job applications. It automatically scans and ranks resumes before a human ever sees them. If your resume doesn't match the ATS criteria, you're out — even if you're perfectly qualified.

🎯 Why does it matter?

Over 98% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS software. The system looks for specific keywords, formatting requirements, and skill matches. A resume that looks great to human eyes can be completely invisible to an ATS if it's not optimized correctly.

🔑 How to beat it?

Use clean, simple formatting. Mirror the job description's keywords naturally. Avoid tables, graphics, and fancy fonts. Use standard section headings. ByteHearts analyzes all these factors and gives you an exact score with actionable fixes.

📈 What ByteHearts does

We parse your resume the same way ATS software does, compare it against your target job description, score keyword matches, flag formatting issues, and give you specific suggestions to improve — not vague advice, real, actionable changes.
